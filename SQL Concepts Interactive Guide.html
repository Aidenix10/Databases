1. Joins (Side-by-Side)

Connecting lists based on a relationship (e.g., Friends).

Inner Join

Exclusive

Keep only rows that match in both lists.

The Analogy: "The Mutual Friends Party." You only get in if you are on the Left List AND the Right List.

Left Join

V.I.P. Left

Keep everything from the Left list.

The Analogy: Everyone on the Left List gets in. If they have a friend on the Right, the friend comes too.

Right Join

V.I.P. Right

Keep everything from the Right list.

The Analogy: Everyone on the Right List gets in. If they have a friend on the Left, great!

Full Outer Join

Everyone

Keep everything from both lists.

The Analogy: "Everyone is Invited." Left comes, Right comes. If they match, they stand together. If not, they stand alone.

Self Join

Mirror

Comparing a list to itself.

The Analogy: Finding pairs inside the same group (e.g., employees who live in the same city as their boss).

Cartesian Product

Cross Join

Every possible combination.

The Analogy: "Mix & Match." Every shirt paired with every pair of pants.

2. Unions (The Aggregators)

Use this when you want to stack lists on top of each other.

Think: "I have a list of Apples and a list of Oranges. I want one big list of Fruit."

Command Logic The Lego Analogy
Union
Clean List
 Combine + Remove Duplicates You dump two buckets of Legos. If you find two identical red bricks, you throw one away and keep just one.
Union All
Raw List
 Combine + Keep Everything You dump two buckets of Legos and keep the pile exactly as it is. If you have five identical red bricks, you keep all five.

3. Intersects (The Matchmakers)

Use this when you want to find matches between lists.

Think: "I have a list of Students and a list of Athletes. Who is on BOTH lists?"

Command Logic The Lego Analogy
Intersect
Shared Only
 Match + Remove Duplicates You look at both buckets. You only pick out the pieces that exist in Bucket A AND Bucket B.
Intersect All
Shared w/ Dups
 Match + Keep Duplicates You check both buckets. If Bucket A has 3 red bricks and Bucket B has 2, you keep the 2 that match in both places.

Cheat Sheet

When should you actually use these?

Use Union when:

You need a Clean List.

  • Example: Mailing Lists.
  • Why? You don't want to email the same person twice just because they signed up twice.

Use Union All when:

You need Total Accuracy or Speed.

  • Example: Bank Statements.
  • Why? If you deposit $5 twice, you want to see it twice. You don't want the bank to merge them!

Use Intersect when:

You need to find Common Ground.

  • Example: Marketing.
  • Why? "Which customers bought an iPhone AND an iPad?" You don't care how many, just who.

Use Intersect All when:

You need Inventory Matching.

  • Example: Card Trading.
  • Why? I have 2 Charizards, you have 3. We have exactly 2 cards in common to trade. Quantity matters.
