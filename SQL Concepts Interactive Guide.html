Types of Joins

Connecting lists side-by-side (The "Party" Analogies)

Left Join

V.I.P. Left

Keep everything from the Left list.

The Analogy: Imagine a party where everyone on the Left List is a VIP. They get in no matter what. If they have a friend on the Right list, the friend comes too. If not, they enter alone.

Right Join

V.I.P. Right

Keep everything from the Right list.

The Analogy: The opposite of the Left Join. Everyone on the Right List is a VIP. They get in automatically. If they have a friend on the Left, great! If not, they still get in.

Full Outer Join

Everyone

Keep everything from both lists.

The Analogy: The "Everyone is Invited" party. Everyone from the Left comes. Everyone from the Right comes. If they match, they stand together. If not, they stand alone. Nobody is left out.

Self Join

Mirror

Comparing a list to itself.

The Analogy: You have a list of students. You want to find pairs who live in the same city. You aren't bringing in a second list; you are looking for connections inside the same group.

Cartesian Product

Cross Join

Combine every row from Left with every row from Right.

The Analogy: "The Mix & Match." Imagine you have 3 shirts and 2 pairs of pants. You try every shirt with every pair of pants to see every possible outfit. It creates a massive list of combinations!

Combining Data

Stacking lists on top of each other (The "Lego" Analogies)

Term Definition The Lego Analogy
Union
No Duplicates
 Combine two lists, remove duplicates. You dump two buckets of Legos. If you find two identical red bricks, you throw one away and keep just one.
Union All
Keep All
 Combine two lists, keep everything. You dump two buckets of Legos and keep the pile exactly as it is. If you have five identical red bricks, you keep all five.
Intersect
Shared Only
 Find items appearing in both lists. You look at both buckets. You only pick out the pieces that exist in Bucket A AND Bucket B.
Join
General
 Connecting data side-by-side. Sticking two stickers together. You have a sticker with a name and another with a grade. You stick them together because they belong to the same person.
