üìñ Dictionary & Legends (What do these terms mean?)

Understanding these terms will save you money.

Principal Cardholder The "Boss" of the account. You are responsible for paying all bills, including those made by your Supplementary cards.
Supplementary Card An extra card given to a family member. It shares your credit limit, but you (the Principal) pay the bill.
Finance Charge (FC) Another name for Interest. This is the extra money you pay the bank if you don't pay the entire bill by the due date.
Minimum Amount Due The smallest amount you must pay to keep your account "active."
Warning: Paying only this means you will be charged huge interest on the rest.
SOA (Statement of Account) Your monthly bill. It lists everything you bought in the last 30 days.
Cash Advance Using your credit card at an ATM to get cash.
Avoid this! Fees and interest start immediately (no grace period).
ADA (Auto-Debit Arrangement) A setup where the bank automatically takes the payment from your savings account so you don't forget.
Installment Availment Converting a big purchase into smaller monthly payments.

4. Important Interest Rules

The "Golden" Number: 3.00%
This is the monthly interest rate.

When do you get charged Interest?

  1. If you pay nothing by the due date.
  2. If you pay less than the Total Amount Due (even if you pay the Minimum).
  3. If you carry over a balance from the previous month.
IMPORTANT REMINDER:
Paying less than the total amount increases your debt quickly because interest is charged on your entire balance, not just the part you didn't pay.
